The making of Daft Punk's One More Time

This wonderfully animated clip shows how to get the main loop of Daft Punk's One More Time from Eddie Johns' More Spell On You. There's a lot going on in the animation and it proves that, if done right, visual communication can pack in an awful lot of information in just a few seconds. I found the following "visual vocabulary" quite innovative:

  • highlighting sections of the original song showing us which bits were chopped out
  • the white line that shows us the point that is currently playing in the sample
  • the stretching of the sample which has the effect of lowering the frequency

There's 36 more videos just like it which I'm looking forward to watching.

Making Melbourne's Brunswick St more bike friendly

I rode along Brunswick St many times in the year that I lived in Melbourne. I can personally attest to how dangerous it felt. I was in a traffic limbo, caught between the threat of car doors being opened on me to my left and vehicles 10-20 times my mass plowing into me from the right.

Hydroxychloroquine and how it became a political hot potato

I was following hydroxychloroquine as a potential treatment for COVID-19 before Trump had showed any support for it. I even donated to a Queensland trial of the drug. But everything changed once Trump declared his support for the drug. All of a sudden it became highly politicised. People on the right touted it as a miracle cure, while people on the left cast as much doubt on it as they could.

The truth was somewhere in between as you'll discover in Norman Doidge's article. Other works by him include the books The Brain That Changes Itself and The Brain's Way of Healing.
