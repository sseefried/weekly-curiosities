Bullying, trading in outrage and the locust economy

Mon 01 Mar 2021

Most teen bullying occurs among peers climbing social ladder

In school I was both bullied and a bully. I remember being told (by well-meaning adults) that the reason bullies lash out is because of a lack of self esteem or because of problems at home. Apart from the fact that bullies appear to have anything but a lack of self-esteem, what if this is only part of the story?

Intuitively, mosty of us recognise that there was a social ladder-climbing aspect to bullying. Who can deny that making fun of someone didn't help but rub off well on the person making fun, even if we felt bad for feeling that way? I can distinctly remember how charismatic and funny it made them seem.

The article below shares some research showing that much of the bullying seems to occur among friends who are trying to climb the social ladder. The paper also presents evidence that most anti-bullying programs don't work. This also accords with my experience. I distinctly remember how we were schooled in how to recognise and call out bullying but, after just a few days, or even hours!, we would be using the new vocabulary in the most sarcastic way with each other, a metaphorical nod and wink to the fact that we all knew it wouldn't have any meaningful effect.

It makes you wonder though, if you had the right foundation for understanding bullying, one that recognised it wasn't just kids with problems at home that did it, whether one might be able to craft some solutions that actually worked.

The Locust Economy

Remember when Groupon was a big thing? Did you start to realise that the businesses you were getting great deals from were often struggling financially and struggling even more to honour the deal they had just given? Did it start to make you feel guilty? You may have been, just as I was, part of the Locust Economy, a metaphorical plague from the skies stripping small businesses down to nothing.
