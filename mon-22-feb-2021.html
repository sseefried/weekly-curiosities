Paying everyone the same salary doesn't work

I believe in progress even if we don't always achieve it. Because of this my natural inclination is to reflexively question tradition and discard it when appropriate. However, within the last decade I've come to realise just how hard it is to change things for the better. e.g. Facebook has not be a nett good for society.

A lot of our society's ways of doing things have deep reasons for being that are not immediately apparent. This can be summed up by the adage, due to Donald Kingsbury, that tradition is a set of solutions for which we have forgotten the problems. This is why it is particularly instructional to see examples of situations where someone discards tradition and quickly discovers why that tradition existed in the first place.

The article below documents an experiment where a company tried to pay all of its employees the same salary regardless of experience or how motivated each employee was. The experiment had to be abandoned fairly quickly once it was clear it was sowing discord among the employees.
